Event Summary

Welcome to the ZoeCare 'STACHE DASH Road & Trail Race. The 1ST Annual 3, 5 & 10k 'STACHE DASH will support ZoeCare's Man UP and WAIT Training programs.

The 'STACHE DASH couples perfectly with these new programs. Man UP has embarked upon a fatherhood initiative and male mentoring campaign entitled "Taking Back the Beard." An unplanned pregnancy can be as unsettling for a new 'father to be' as it is for young mothers. Man UP helps men find their voice in unplanned pregnancies. We offer mentoring and parenting tools, what masculinity means and an opportunity for men to step up and into their new fatherhood role.

WAIT Training is a relationship training curriculum designed for 7th through 9th grade public school students. It helps young people gain relationship skills and tools and addresses issues like bullying, sexting, pornography, self-esteem, boundaries and sexual integrity.

Event Details

The 'STACHE DASH consists of three separate distances: 3K Walk, and 5K & 10K runs. Race[s] begin at Bogert Park, 9am, Saturday, June 28. The course covers trail on both Pete's Hill and the Bozeman Trail system.

Runners of every age, shape and size, boys and girls are WELCOME.

Mustaches are very much encouraged (real or fake). The mustache competition is open to everyone.

Two water stations will be available on course and at the finish line.

Portable restrooms will be available at Bogert Park.

Packet Pick-up

Early race packet pick-up is strongly encouraged. Packets may be picked up at Bozeman Running Company on Friday, June 27. Packets will be available prior to the race for those unable to pick up a packet on Friday at BRC. Once again, getting your packet early is strongly encouraged. The races will be staggered beginning with the 5k, 10 minute stagger to the 10k followed 10 minutes later by the 3k walk. The race will START promptly at 9am.

Post Race

Awards will be given immediately following the race. Beverages and snacks will be available.
