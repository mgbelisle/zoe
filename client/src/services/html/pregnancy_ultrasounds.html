What is an ultrasound?

A prenatal ultrasound test uses high-frequency sound waves, inaudible to the human ear, that are transmitted through the abdomen via a device called a transducer to look at the inside of the abdomen. With prenatal ultrasound, the echoes are recorded and transformed into video or photographic images of your baby.

The ultrasound can be used during pregnancy to show images of the baby, amniotic sac, placenta, and ovaries. Major anatomical abnormalities or birth defects are visible on an ultrasound.

Most prenatal ultrasound procedures are performed topically, or on the surface of the skin, using a gel as a conductive medium to aid in the image quality. However, a transvaginal ultrasound is an alternative procedure in which a tubular probe is inserted into the vaginal canal. This method of ultrasound produces an image quality that is greatly enhanced, but it is not a common prenatal procedure. However, it may be used early in pregnancy to get a clearer view of the uterus or ovaries if a problem is suspected. It may also be used early in pregnancy to determine how far along you are in your pregnancy (gestational age).

Is prenatal ultrasound safe?

Studies have shown ultrasound is not hazardous. There are no harmful side effects to you or your baby. In addition, ultrasound does not use radiation, as X-ray tests do.

Answers to Many of Your Questions

If you tested positive on a ZoeCare administered pregnancy test you may receive a free limited ultrasound if the estimated gestational age is at least 6 to 7 weeks. If you are underage, parental consent may be required to perform an ultrasound. Some medical considerations may preclude or postpone an ultrasound. An ultrasound can determine if you have a viable pregnancy and the gestational age (how far along you are).

We do a limited ultrasound here at ZoeCare that provides the following information:

  • Verify a pregnancy
  • Confirm that the pregnancy is in the uterus, and is not an ectopic, tubal pregnancy or a pregnancy located outside of the womb
  • Take measurements of the baby to see how far along you are
  • Measure the baby's heart rate to determine a viable pregnancy

A diagnostic ultrasound is performed at 20 weeks and is more involved. It is ordered and done by your health care provider and not by ZoeCare.

What is an ectopic pregnancy? Do I need to know if I have an ectopic pregnancy?

An ultrasound is also a diagnostic tool for determining whether or not a pregnancy is safe. A fertilized egg in the fallopian tubes (an ectopic pregnancy) or outside of the womb is not safe. A woman diagnosed with an ectopic pregnancy needs to seek emergency medical attention. If your ultrasounds reveals signs of an ectopic pregnancy, you will be referred by our staff to seek immediate medical attention.

How can I know when I got pregnant?

If you don't remember the date of your last period or are not sure when you may have conceived, an ultrasound can provide an accurate measurement of the age or your baby. It is very important to know how far along you are in your pregnancy if you are considering an abortion. Gestational age of the baby will determine what abortion methods are available to you and enable you to know the risks and possible long term effects associated with each prescribed method.
