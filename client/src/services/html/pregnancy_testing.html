Pregnancy Testing

ZoeCare offers free pregnancy tests that are 99% accurate. You will be provided with the result of your pregnancy test including a verification form you may take with you. If you have a positive pregnancy test, you may schedule a free ultrasound to determine the status of your pregnancy.

There are three viable pregnancy tests, including:

1. Urine Testing
Urine Testing is offered free at ZoeCare. Home testing can be done with the purchase of a pregnancy testing kit at drug stores, most grocery stores, and some department stores. The cost is about $10 to $20. Accuracy varies depending on how long it has been since your last menstrual period and the accuracy of the test. The urine testing at ZoeCare is 99% accurate, even if your menstrual period is one day late. The test can sometimes indicate a pregnancy prior to noticing a late period.
2. Blood Testing
The blood test is called serum beta hCG. It measures the same hormone that the urine test measures. The cost is about $50. It will indicate a pregnancy only a day or two sooner than the urine testing. A serum beta hCG test must be ordered by a physician. To be completely accurate in determining a viable (heart motion and/or living embryo) pregnancy, two blood tests must be done about two or three days apart, which doubles the cost. ZoeCare offers this testing at no charge, but only after certain criteria have been met. Those criteria can only be determined by an ultrasound. This is very rare. Blood testing for routine pregnancy confirmation is not medically indicated.
3. Ultrasound

ZoeCare offers free limited obstetrical ultrasounds with the medical purpose of confirming a viable pregnancy. A limited ultrasound is recommended because the urine test can be positive for up to two weeks even when a miscarriage has occurred. An ultrasound is a medical procedure using sound waves to view a pregnancy. It is painless and has been used in obstetrics for over 40 years without harm or ill effect to mother or baby.

If you are considering an abortion an ultrasound will answer two important questions:

  1. It will confirm a viable pregnancy. This is important because there is no need to pay for an abortion, or undergo the emotional and physical trauma experienced by many women who abort, if you have incurred a natural miscarriage. One in five early pregnancies end in a natural miscarriage.
  2. An ultrasound will provide accurate dating of the pregnancy. Accurate age of the baby is important when you are considering an abortion, because it will help determine what type of abortion is medically necessary. There may be a considerable difference in cost between a chemical (medication/pills) and a surgical abortion.

The ultrasounds at ZoeCare are performed by registered nurses with advanced ultrasound (sonographer) training. All ZoeCare ultrasounds are reviewed by a medical doctor.

I already tested posive at home and just want an ultrasound. Now what?
If you have taken a home pregnancy test prior to your appointment and tested positive, we still require you to take a pregnancy test at ZoeCare. Our pregnancy tests are more accurate than most home tests because they are medical quality tests. It is important for us to confirm your pregnancy as well as the approximate gestational age of your child prior to performing an ultrasound test.
