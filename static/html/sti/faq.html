Frequently Asked Questions

Should I be tested?
All sexually active individuals that are not in a monogamous relationship should be tested for a sexually transmitted infection (STI) and/or a sexually transmitted disease (STD). If you are sexually active and have more than one partner you are at risk for STIs. STI’s are very common.
What are the symptoms of STIs?

Symptoms are not always obvious, and often there are no symptoms at all. Even if you have no symptoms, you can still pass the infection to other partners. Women are more likely NOT to have symptoms. If left untreated, the STI (sexual transmitted infection) can become more severe and also increase your risk of acquiring another STI.

Some general symptoms include:

  • Painful or burning sensation when urinating
  • Thick, cloudy, or bloody discharge from penis or vagina
  • Pain during sexual intercourse in women
  • Painful or swollen testicles in men
  • Itching in the genital area, vagina, penis, or anal area
  • Lesions like warts or ulcers in genital area
  • Sore throat
  • Fever
  • Fatigue

Remember there are no symptoms in infected persons 50% of the time. The percentage is higher for women.

How are STIs spread?
STI's are spread through skin to skin contact, vaginal, anal and oral sex. Skin to skin contact, does not mean just walking up to someone and touching them. Skin to skin contact means coming in contact with an infected area. HPV (Human Papilloma Virus) and Herpes are spread through skin to skin contact, this means actual sexual intercourse is not required to contract Herpes or HPV. Other STIs like Chlamydia, Gonorrhea, Syphilis, HIV, Hepatitis B are ALL spread through bodily fluids like blood, urine, semen, and vaginal fluid. These fluids are exchanged during oral, vaginal and anal sex.
How many STI’s are there?
There are about 40 different kinds of STI’s, however, several of them have many various strains.
