Verify Your Pregnancy

Before making an appointment for any type of abortion, you will need to verify you are actually pregnant. You will also need to know how far along you are in the pregnancy. Even if you are experiencing pregnancy symptoms or have taken a home pregnancy test that was positive, you should get a medically accurate, lab-quality pregnancy test. You can get a free lab-quality pregnancy test at ZoeCare. Our tests are 99% accurate.

The Abortion Pill, RU-486

It’s important to know the facts before you undergo a chemical abortion via RU-486.* RU-486, like other drugs, has potentially serious side effects and complications.

You should not take RU-486 if you have:

  • high blood pressure
  • heart disease
  • bleeding problem[s]
  • anemia
  • uncontrolled diabetes

Complications

The FDA has released reports of complications including death resulting from hemorrhage (excessive bleeding), serious and sometimes fatal infections, and missed diagnoses of ectopic pregancy.

Before taking RU-486 make sure you follow the two recommendations made by the FDA.

Get an ultrasound to determine if the pregnancy is in the uterus. RU-486 has no effect on an ectopic pregnancy which can be fatal if undetected.

Make sure you have access to Emergency Care including a surgical facility in case an emergency surgical abortion becomes necessary.

If you cannot get an ultrasound prior to taking RU-486 and you do not have access to emergency medical care, you are at even greater risk. Ignoring the use guidelines for RU-486 makes this choice much less safe.

Plan B

The Morning After Pill / Emergency Contraception

Plan B is an emergency contraceptive, available over the counter and taken by mouth after unprotected sex. It is used to prevent pregnancy. Plan B does not protect you against an STD/STI (sexually transmitted disease and/or infection).
